Who Am I?

Hi, I'm Naveen Porwal, a Ph.D. researcher at IISER Bhopal, specializing in the field of robotics and automation. My main area of focus is multi-agent reinforcement learning, particularly in the context of generalized persistent monitoring by a team of cooperative multi-agent systems.

My work is applied to a range of real-world problems, such as border patrol and security, where automated systems must work together in real-time to ensure effective monitoring. This research has earned me the honor of being nominated for the prestigious Prime Minister Research Fellowship (PMRF).

My Background

I completed both my Bachelor's and Master's in Physics at IISER Bhopal. During my Master's thesis, I worked on theoretical high-energy physics and its intersection with machine learning. This foundational work set the stage for my current research interests in applied machine learning and robotics.

I am driven by a passion for learning and technology. Whether it's coding, experimenting with new robotics techniques, or diving into the latest advancements in machine learning, I thrive in the world of innovation. Outside of my academic life, I enjoy cycling, playing basketball, and learning new languages to expand my horizons.
